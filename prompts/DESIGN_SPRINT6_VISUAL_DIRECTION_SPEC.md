# Design Worker — Sprint 6 Visual Direction Spec

## Mission
Lock the visual direction for Sprint 6 so content and engineering can execute without style drift or product ambiguity.

## You are defining
1. the final art-style statement,
2. the slice-zone priority order,
3. the material / palette / signage rules,
4. the UI skin direction,
5. the event visual language,
6. the proof standard QA will use.

## Required decisions
- Exact visual-style sentence for the game
- Priority order for the five polish zones
- What absolutely must stop looking like primitive graybox in this sprint
- Palette tokens and where each token is allowed
- Material rules by zone family
- Signage language and typography direction
- HUD skin direction and alert treatment
- Blackout look rules
- Mimic look rules
- Success / failure summary look rules
- Asset-source policy:
  - Studio-built only,
  - Studio-first with optional imports,
  - or another explicit rule
- Performance guardrails for effects, lights, and clutter
- Hero shot / thumbnail direction

## Constraints
- Do not introduce new gameplay systems
- Preserve readability on phone-sized UI
- Prefer modular reuse over bespoke hero art
- Keep the horror readable and platform-appropriate
- Do not require a full custom asset pipeline for Sprint 6 success

## Files to update
- `project/docs/GDD.md`
- `project/docs/ART-DIRECTION.md`
- `project/docs/DECISIONS.md`
- `project/docs/HANDOFF-ENGINEERING.md`
- `project/docs/HANDOFF-CONTENT.md`

## Engineering handoff must include
- palette / material table
- lighting-state intent by mode
- UI component treatment list
- zone-priority list
- must-fix primitive replacement matrix
- performance guardrails
- QA proof checklist

## Definition of done
Engineering and content should both be unblocked after your handoff with no core style ambiguity left.
