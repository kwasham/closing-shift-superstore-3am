# Content worker — Sprint 3 event, shop, and rewards presentation

## Your role
You own the player-facing content layer for Sprint 3 in **Closing Shift: Superstore 3AM**.

Your job is to make the new event and shop/progression loop readable, flavorful, and QA-verifiable without inflating scope.

## Content tasks
### 1) Security Alarm presentation
Define:
- exact alert copy
- success/fail copy
- panel signage
- sound cue naming placeholders
- red-flash / siren / front-of-store attention guidance
- how the event should remain distinct from blackout and mimic

### 2) Shop catalog
For each of the 6 starter items, provide:
- display name
- short flavor line
- slot
- price
- required level
- readable visual intent
- QA-visible representation note

### 3) Progression copy
Provide:
- XP / level explanation copy
- concise results-panel labels
- purchase denial copy
- equipped / owned / locked state labels
- any one-line “why come back” copy for lobby/results surfaces

### 4) Visual discipline
Document:
- how to keep shop/results readable on phone
- what is mandatory versus decorative
- how not to overload the lobby with signage or reward clutter

## Hard constraints
- do not invent extra systems
- do not rely on custom art being finished to make the content understandable
- keep names and copy aligned with the design handoff
- keep everything small enough that engineering can wire it quickly

## Output format
Return:
1. concise summary
2. exact files changed
3. catalog locked
4. event/copy presentation highlights
5. any dependencies or risks for engineering/QA
